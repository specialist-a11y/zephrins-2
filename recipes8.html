Baker's Wisdom

Baking & Storage Tips

Keep your loaves tasting as fresh as the moment they left our ovens.

Because we bake without artificial preservatives, store your bread at room temperature in its original plastic bag or a bread box. Avoid storing in the refrigerator as this causes the bread to dry out and go stale faster. Use or freeze within 3–4 days.

Absolutely! Our loaves and rolls freeze beautifully. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or keep them in their original bag, then place them in the freezer for up to 3 months. Slice the loaf before freezing so you can easily toast individual slices directly from the freezer.

To bring back that fresh-baked crust and soft interior, preheat your oven or toaster oven to 350°F (175°C). Mist the roll lightly with a little water (or wrap in a damp paper towel for 10 seconds in the microwave first), then bake in the oven for 5 to 7 minutes. It will taste brand new!

Bajan Classic

Bajan Flying Fish Cutter

Yield: 2 Cutters Prep: 15 Mins Cook: 10 Mins

Ingredients

  • 2 Zephirin's Salt Bread rolls
  • 4 fresh flying fish fillets (or red snapper/sea bass fillets)
  • 2 tbsp Barbadian seasoning (herb paste)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup fine breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Lettuce leaves, sliced tomatoes, Cheddar cheese (optional)
  • Bajan hot sauce & mayonnaise

Directions

  1. Season the fish fillets with lime juice, then rub the Bajan seasoning paste thoroughly into both sides. Let marinate for at least 15 minutes.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat (about 350°F / 175°C).
  3. Dip each fillet into the beaten egg, then dredge in breadcrumbs until fully coated.
  4. Fry the fillets for 2–3 minutes on each side until golden-brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Slice your Zephirin's Salt Bread rolls in half. Spread mayonnaise on the bottom roll, lay in the crispy fish, add cheese, lettuce, and tomatoes if desired, and finish with a heavy dash of Bajan hot sauce. Close the sandwich and serve immediately.
Breakfast Specialty

Spiced Raisin Bread French Toast

Yield: 2 Servings Prep: 5 Mins Cook: 10 Mins

Ingredients

  • 4 thick slices of Zephirin's Spiced Raisin Bread
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup whole milk or heavy cream
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of freshly grated nutmeg
  • 2 tbsp butter for the griddle
  • Barbadian sugar cane molasses or maple syrup for serving
  • Fresh berries or sliced bananas (optional)

Directions

  1. In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla, cinnamon, and nutmeg until fully blended.
  2. Melt 1 tablespoon of butter in a large skillet or griddle over medium heat.
  3. Dip each slice of Zephirin's Raisin Bread into the egg mixture, allowing it to soak for 10–15 seconds on each side. Do not let it get too soggy.
  4. Place the soaked slices onto the hot skillet. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through. Melt more butter as needed.
  5. Transfer to plates. Serve warm topped with fresh fruit and a generous drizzle of local sugar cane molasses.
Modern Twist

Toasted Coconut Bread w/ Crushed Avocado

Yield: 2 Toast Slices Prep: 5 Mins Cook: 5 Mins

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of Zephirin's Sweet Coconut Bread
  • 1 ripe Barbadian avocado
  • Juice of 1/2 lime
  • 1/2 tsp red chili flakes
  • Flaky sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil (optional)
  • Coriander (cilantro) leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. Cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Add the lime juice, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of black pepper. Mash with a fork until desired texture (chunky or smooth) is reached.
  2. Lightly toast the Coconut Sweet Bread slices in a toaster or on a dry skillet over medium-high heat for 2 minutes per side until the edges are crispy and caramelized.
  3. Spread the mashed avocado evenly over the toasted coconut sweet bread.
  4. Drizzle a tiny amount of olive oil on top, then sprinkle with red chili flakes, flaky sea salt, and fresh coriander leaves. Serve warm.
